Hoda Fahime Booles

Scientific Advisor, Molecular Biology & Life Science Research

Hoda Fahime Booles is an Egyptian scientist and professor specializing in cell biology, genetics, toxicology, and molecular biology. She serves as Professor in the Department of Cell Biology at the National Research Centre in Giza, Egypt, where she leads research focused on genetics, cytogenetics, DNA damage and repair mechanisms, oxidative stress, and reproductive toxicology.

Her scientific work covers several important areas within modern life sciences, including diabetes research, Alzheimer's disease, cancer biology, and the investigation of medicinal plants and natural products. Her research explores biomarkers for forensic applications, bioactive plant-derived compounds, natural antioxidants, and mechanisms related to DNA protection, inflammation, and cellular health.

With more than 30 peer-reviewed scientific publications, Hoda has contributed extensively to advancing knowledge in molecular and cellular biology. Her research has gained international visibility, supporting collaboration and knowledge exchange across the life science community.

Through her scientific expertise, Hoda provides valuable insight into emerging life science trends, research-driven innovation, and the scientific foundations supporting next-generation healthcare and biotechnology solutions.
